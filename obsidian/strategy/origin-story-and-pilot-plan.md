# Origin Story & Pilot Plan

## The Opportunity

Chris's wife is a lead RDH at a practice whose owners operate ~110 practices nationally and are also VCs. This creates a rare founder advantage:

- **Embedded domain expert** — a lead RDH with daily insight into how treatment plans get presented, dropped, and followed up
- **Warm intro** to a real practice through a trusted relationship
- **Built-in champion** who can advocate internally
- **Direct line to VC owners** who could deploy across 110 locations (~$395K ARR at $299/mo)

## 90-Day Pilot Strategy

1. **Weeks 1-4: Finish integrations & polish** — Twilio, Resend, [[pms-connector-architecture|PMS connector]] for the pilot practice's system. Staging environment testing with wife walking through every workflow as front desk staff
2. **Weeks 4-8: White-glove pilot** — One practice, hands-on setup. Instrument everything: revenue recovered, response rates, time to book. Build a clean before/after story
3. **Weeks 8-12: Let results speak** — Don't pitch VC owners until 60-90 days of real data exist. Then ask for a 3-4 practice expansion, not a full rollout. Let them escalate it themselves

## Key Risk

Wife's professional relationship is the foundation. The product must be genuinely ready before it enters that practice — not "good for a beta," actually ready.

## Product Status

~90% complete. Service integrations and staging testing remain. Sandbox demo environment is live for dentists to preview the product.

## Expansion Path

1. Pilot results (1 practice, 60-90 days)
2. Small expansion (3-5 locations from the same group)
3. Portfolio conversation (110-location rollout)
4. VC relationship / potential funding

## The Sandbox as Sales Tool

Instead of pitch decks, fire up the [[sandbox-auth-signup-flow|sandbox]] — simulation engine runs, booking events fire, the revenue counter ticks up, activity feed is live. A visceral, memorable demo that no slides can replicate.
