# Investor Pitch Narrative

> The story arc for conversations with the VC practice owners and future investors. Not a slide deck — a narrative framework that weaves the vault's data into a compelling pitch. Adapt for audience: practice owners care about ROI, VCs care about market size and moat.

---

## The Opening (30 Seconds)

"Every dental practice in the country has half a million dollars in treatment plans that patients said yes to but never scheduled. That's $89 billion sitting on the table industry-wide. We built the product that recovers it — automatically, with zero front desk effort."

---

## The Problem (2 Minutes)

The ADA confirms the average dental practice sustains **$500K-$1M in unscheduled treatment plans**. Treatment plan acceptance is 56%, but completion drops to 46% — a 10% leakage that no existing tool closes.

Why? Because every tool on the market — Weave, Dental Intelligence, RevenueWell — generates a **call list** for front desk staff to manually work through. Front desk staff are busy, underpaid, and turn over constantly. The call list doesn't get worked. The revenue doesn't get recovered.

See [[market-size-and-opportunity]] for full TAM/SAM/SOM breakdown.

---

## The Solution (2 Minutes)

Retaine is a **revenue recovery engine** — not a communication platform, not a practice management system.

A practice connects their PMS. Retaine reads their unscheduled treatment plans and automatically fires multi-step follow-up sequences — personalized SMS on day 3, email on day 10, voicemail on day 21 — with **zero front desk intervention**.

The messages are generated by Claude (AI), tuned to the specific procedure, tone, and step in the sequence. They don't feel like templates. They feel like a thoughtful follow-up from the practice.

When a patient responds, the inbox shows the reply with **AI-detected intent** — "wants to book," "has a question," "not ready." Staff see a priority queue, not raw messages.

**Live demo available** — the [[sandbox-auth-signup-flow|sandbox]] shows a realistic practice dashboard with simulation running in real time.

---

## Why Now (2 Minutes)

This problem is 20 years old. The tools to solve it converged in the last 24 months:

1. **AI personalization** — LLM-generated, procedure-aware messages didn't exist before 2023. "Hi Susan, Dr. Rodriguez wanted to follow up about your crown" at scale is genuinely new
2. **HIPAA-compliant SMS** — Twilio now offers BAAs and encrypted messaging. This was a legal minefield 3 years ago
3. **PMS middleware** — Services like Kolla and DentalBridge provide a single API across Dentrix, Eaglesoft, and Open Dental. Integration that used to take 6 months is now a $19/location line item
4. **Cloud-native practices** — 80%+ of practices are now on cloud systems. Post-COVID dentists expect self-serve SaaS

The incumbents can't pivot — their architecture assumes a human in the loop. Rebuilding would mean starting over. See [[why-now-timing-analysis]] for the full analysis.

## The Business Model (1 Minute)

**$299/month flat per practice.** No per-seat, no per-message, no hidden fees.

A practice recovers ~$150,000/year from a $500K backlog at a 30% conversion rate. Retaine costs $3,588/year. That's a **42x ROI** — it pays for itself with the first recovered crown.

Margins are exceptional: **57% gross margin at 10 practices, 90%+ at 100 practices.** The fixed compliance costs (Supabase HIPAA, Twilio Enterprise) get amortized fast, and variable costs (SMS, AI, email) are trivial per-practice.

See [[unit-economics-and-scaling-costs]] for full cost breakdown at every stage.

## The Unfair Advantage (1 Minute)

1. **Embedded domain expert** — co-founder's wife is a lead RDH at the pilot practice. She shaped every workflow, every message template, every UX decision from daily clinical experience
2. **110-practice distribution path** — the pilot practice's owners operate 110 locations nationally and are VCs. A successful pilot becomes a portfolio rollout conversation, not a cold sales cycle
3. **Data moat building from day one** — every sequence that runs generates conversion intelligence: which messages work for which procedures, at which day offsets, in which tones. That dataset is not replicable by a competitor who launches 18 months later

## Competitive Positioning (1 Minute)

"Every competitor in this space is a **communication platform** that bolted on treatment follow-up as a feature. We're a **revenue recovery engine** that does one thing and does it better than anyone."

| What We Do | What They Do |
|-----------|-------------|
| AI-generated, procedure-aware messages | Static templates with `{first_name}` |
| Autonomous multi-step sequences | Manual call lists for staff |
| Revenue recovered as primary metric | Communication metrics |
| 10-minute self-serve onboarding | Multi-week implementation |
| Modern NextJS + Shadcn UI | Windows 2003 WinForms aesthetic |

The most direct threat is **Oryx** (modern cloud PMS, Inc. 5000 ranked), which could build this as a feature in 18-36 months. Our moat is the conversion data we accumulate before they do. See [[competitive-landscape]] for the full breakdown.
